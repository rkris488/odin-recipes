Fried Dumplings Skirt

Crispy on the bottom, moist on top

This recipe creates a beautiful lattice crust on the dumplings while maintaining a moist and juicy steamed dumpling on the top. It's my go-to midnight snack.

INGREDIENTS:

Makes 40 Dumplings

Filling:

  • 250g ground pork (can use ground chicken or drained tofu!)
  • ½ Nappa cabbage, thinly sliced
  • 1 tbsp kosher salt
  • 1 tsp minced ginger
  • 1 tbsp minced garlic
  • ½ tsp white pepper (can be substituted with black pepper)
  • 1 tsp sesame oil
  • 1 tbsp neutral oil

Assembly/Frying:

  • 1 pack gyoza wrappers
  • 2 tsp AP flour
  • 2 tbsp neutral oil

Slurry:

  • 1 tsp corn starch
  • 75mL or ⅓ cup cold water

Dipping Sauce:

  • 1 tsp light soy sauce
  • 1 tsp chili oil
  • ½ tsp minced garlic (optional)

STEPS:

Filling:

  1. Transfer the thinly sliced napa cabbage into a big bowl.

  2. Add salt and massage the cabbages and let them sit for 10 minutes.

  3. After 10 minutes, there should be moisture coming out from the cabbage. Squeeze the cabbages for excess moisture and drain.

  4. Add the pork, ginger, garlic, white pepper, sesame oil, and neutral oil.

  5. Mix until a homogenous mixture forms.

Pleating:

  1. To pleat the dumplings, wet half of the circumference of the wrapper.

  2. Add ½ tbsp of your filling in the center. I like to use a measuring spoon because it makes lovely little mounds!

  3. Fold the wrappers in half. Pinch the center of the dumpling and create two pleats on either side of the dumplings.

  4. Press the seams firmly to make sure the dumplings do not fall apart. You’ll get better with practice— don’t worry!

  5. Place your dumplings on a lightly floured baking tray.

Frying:

  1. Heat your pan on medium-high.

  2. Once the pan has come up to temperature, drizzle in oil.

  3. Place the dumplings in a circular motion.

  4. Fry uncovered for 2-3 minutes or until the bottom is lightly golden brown.

  5. Once the bottom is lightly golden brown, add your slurry.

  6. Close the lid and drop the heat to a medium-low.

  7. Cook anywhere between 5-10 minutes, depending on whether you have fresh dumplings or frozen dumplings.

  8. Once the dumpling skin looks translucent, open the lid and cook out any excess water to crisp up the wing!

  9. At this time, add an extra drizzle of neutral oil to help the wing release from the pan easily.

  10. Once the wing is golden brown, turn the heat off. Use a spatula to make sure that the gyozas have separated from the pan.

  11. Place a plate on the pan and flip.

  12. Enjoy with sauce!
